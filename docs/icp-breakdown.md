# ICP Category Breakdown

**Shared profile across all categories:** European industrial B2B company, 5-50 white collar employees, up to 250 total. Founder-led (founder often 55+), sells physical products or services to factories and warehouses. Capped market. No CRM, no marketing function, prospecting via trade shows / associations / word of mouth.

---

## Category 1 — Industrial Equipment Manufacturers

**Who:** Racking & storage systems, industrial doors, conveyors, packaging lines, lifting equipment, industrial cooling/HVAC.

**When they enter a project:** Late-middle — after the building exists or is being fitted out. Equipment decisions happen 3-12 months after the building permit.

**Key problems:**
- Replacement cycles are 10-20 years: lose the initial fit-out and you're locked out for a generation
- Greenfield projects (new warehouses/factories) are invisible to them until a competitor's trucks show up
- Incumbent suppliers get the extension/renewal business by default — nobody re-tenders a supplier relationship that works
- Their pipeline is whatever the founder's network surfaces — retiring network = shrinking pipeline

**Highest-value signals:** SITADEL building permits (industrial/warehouse category), ICPE filings, new establishment openings (BODACC).

---

## Category 2 — Building Envelope & Infrastructure Specialists

**Who:** Industrial flooring, roofing/cladding, loading docks, fire protection systems, industrial fencing/security.

**When they enter a project:** Early — they must be in the technical specification written by the engineering firm (maître d'œuvre). If they're not specced, they can't even bid.

**Key problems:**
- The real buyer is the engineering firm/architect, not the site owner — and they don't know which firms are designing which projects
- By the time a project is publicly visible (construction started), the spec is closed
- Winning requires relationships with a small set of prescribers they have no systematic way to identify

**Highest-value signals:** SITADEL permits at *filing* stage (earliest), urban planning data, tender publications (BOAMP) for public industrial buildings. **Account mapping is most critical here** — the agent must surface the engineering firm, not just the owner.

---

## Category 3 — Installers & Technical Integrators

**Who:** Industrial electrical installation, automation/robotics integration, process piping, industrial ventilation, compressed air systems.

**When they enter a project:** Middle — subcontracted by the general contractor, or hired directly for retrofits.

**Key problems:**
- Deal flow depends on general contractors — an opaque, relationship-gated subcontracting chain
- No visibility on which GCs just won which projects (this data exists in DECP/BOAMP awards and nobody uses it)
- Retrofit/modernization demand (their best margin) is invisible: it shows up in hiring patterns and capex signals, not permits

**Highest-value signals:** DECP/BOAMP contract awards (which GC won what), ICPE modifications (process changes = integration work), production hiring surges (France Travail).

---

## Category 4 — Industrial Services & Maintenance

**Who:** Maintenance contracts, industrial cleaning, calibration/metrology, equipment retrofit, energy efficiency services.

**When they enter a project:** Post-commissioning, or at contract renewal of an incumbent.

**Key problems:**
- Contracts churn only at renewal or when something changes at the site (new ops director, new owner, incident) — and those change-moments are invisible to outsiders
- New sites choose their service providers in the first 6 months of operation, then lock in
- Growth = catching sites at commissioning or at a leadership change; today both are pure luck

**Highest-value signals:** New establishment registrations reaching operational stage (BODACC + hiring data), M&A/ownership changes (BODACC/Pappers), site management hiring ("responsable maintenance", "directeur de site" postings = regime change).

---

## Category 5 — Technical Distributors & Industrial Wholesale

**Who:** Industrial supplies, components, PPE, consumables, tooling — recurring low-ticket, high-frequency sales to plant purchasing.

**When they enter a project:** At site opening, then continuously.

**Key problems:**
- Pure relationship business: account base = founder's historical network, aging with him
- A new warehouse opening 20km away can start ordering from a national catalog player before the local distributor even knows it exists
- Zero tooling to detect new sites entering their delivery radius

**Highest-value signals:** New establishment openings (BODACC/Sirene) filtered by geography, warehouse/logistics hiring in the delivery zone.

---

## Cross-Category Insight (product implication)

The agent should **weight signals differently by ICP category**:

| Category | Primary signal | Timing sweet spot |
|---|---|---|
| Equipment manufacturers | Building permits + ICPE | 3-12 months post-permit |
| Envelope specialists | Permits at filing + tenders | Earliest — pre-spec |
| Installers/integrators | Contract awards (DECP) + ICPE modifications | GC just won the job |
| Services & maintenance | Establishment operational + leadership hiring | First 6 months of a site |
| Distributors | New establishments in radius | Opening day |

This is Step 1 of the pipeline (profile extraction): Claude classifies the user into a category from their one-sentence description, and the signal scan + account mapping adapt accordingly. One agent, five prospecting strategies.
