# Market Analysis — Persona Split France & Europe

> Figures from official sources (INSEE, Eurostat, Observatoire BPCE, CRA) as of early 2026. Order-of-magnitude reliable for a pitch; spot-check exact figures before putting them on a slide (verification pointers at the bottom).

## France

| Metric | Figure | Source |
|---|---|---|
| SMEs with 10-249 employees | ~160,000 | INSEE, Les entreprises en France |
| Of which manufacturing (NAF C, incl. industrial maintenance) — "PMI" | ~23,000 | INSEE Esane / Eurostat SBS |
| + industrial wholesale & installation (10-249) | +6,000-10,000 | Eurostat SBS (derivation) |
| **Gross industrial ICP pool** | **~30,000** | derivation |
| **Core ICP** (B2B, sells to factories/warehouses, excl. consumer goods) | **~15,000-20,000** | derivation |

Note on the "5-50 white collar" filter: in industrial firms, white-collar staff is typically 20-40% of headcount, so it maps onto the 20-250 total-employee band — i.e. the PMI population minus the smallest firms.

### The succession wall (the "boomer founder" angle, quantified)

- **1 in 5 French SME leaders is aged 60+** (Observatoire BPCE, cession-transmission)
- ~11% of SME leaders are 66+
- ~15,000 PME/ETI change hands every year in France (BPCE)
- Average age of a business seller: ~61 (CRA)
- **Derivation: 20-25% of ~30k industrial SMBs = ~6,000-7,500 companies facing a succession event within the decade — usually with zero digitized commercial function to hand over.** The founder's network IS the CRM, and it retires with him.

## Europe (EU27)

| Metric | Figure | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Total SMEs | ~24-25 million (99.8% of firms) | EC SME Performance Review |
| Manufacturing enterprises (NACE C) | ~2 million | Eurostat SBS |
| Manufacturing, small (10-49) | ~230,000-260,000 | Eurostat sbs_sc_ovw |
| Manufacturing, medium (50-249) | ~50,000-60,000 | Eurostat sbs_sc_ovw |
| **EU manufacturing SMEs 10-249** | **~300,000** | Eurostat |
| **EU ICP incl. industrial wholesale/services, core fit** | **~150,000-200,000** | derivation |

Country split of the 10-249 manufacturing band: **Germany and Italy lead (~55-65k each), then Poland, France (~23k), Spain (~20k)**. Germany + Italy alone ≈ 40% of the band — the EU expansion story writes itself (both have equivalent open registries: Handelsregister/Bundesanzeiger, Registro Imprese).

## Digital maturity (why the ICP is analog by default)

- ~40% of EU SMEs are below "basic digital intensity" (Eurostat/DESI; EU target is 90% by 2030)
- **~70% of small firms (10-49) have no CRM** (Eurostat isoc_eb_crm; manufacturing below the all-sector average)
- France Num barometer: CRM/customer-database usage ~25-35% among French TPE/PME; industrial SMEs among the least digitized sectors

## Headline pitch numbers

1. **France: ~30,000 industrial SMBs, core ICP 15-20k companies**
2. **Europe: ~300,000 manufacturing SMEs — Germany + Italy = 40% of them**
3. **1 in 5 founders is 60+ — the network that generates their pipeline is retiring**
4. **7 in 10 have no CRM — every existing GTM tool assumes a user that doesn't exist here**
